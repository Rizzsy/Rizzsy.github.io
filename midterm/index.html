Iloilo City's History & Culture

Iloilo City, known as the “Heart of the Philippines” and the “City of Love”, has a rich history dating back to the pre-colonial era when it was a thriving trading hub. During the Spanish period, Iloilo became one of the most important cities in the Philippines, earning the title “La Muy Leal y Noble Ciudad de Iloilo” (The Most Loyal and Noble City) from the Spanish Crown due to its support for Spain during the Philippine Revolution.

The city flourished as a center for the sugar industry in the 19th century, attracting trade and European influences, which can still be seen in its heritage buildings, such as those in Calle Real and Molo Mansion.

Iloilo’s culture is deeply rooted in its Ilonggo heritage, known for its people’s soft-spoken and gentle manner. It is also famous for its vibrant Dinagyang Festival, a grand celebration in honor of the Santo Niño (Child Jesus), showcasing energetic street performances and cultural traditions. Ilonggo cuisine, including the iconic La Paz Batchoy and Pancit Molo, reflects the city’s rich culinary heritage.
